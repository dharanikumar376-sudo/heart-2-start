# **🏍️ HEAD TO START – Smart Helmet Based Motorcycle Ignition System**

## **📌 Project Overview**

**HEAD TO START** is an innovative motorcycle safety system designed to ensure that riders wear a helmet before starting their bike. The system uses a **Force Sensitive Resistor (FSR) sensor**, **Bluetooth communication**, and a **motorcycle control unit** to create a secure and intelligent ignition mechanism.

The primary goal of this project is to **reduce road accidents caused by riders not wearing helmets** and promote safe riding habits through technology.

---

# **🎯 Problem Statement**

Many motorcycle riders ignore helmet safety rules, leading to severe injuries and fatalities during accidents. Existing traffic regulations alone are not always effective in ensuring helmet usage.

**HEAD TO START** solves this issue by making helmet usage mandatory for motorcycle ignition.

---

# **💡 Proposed Solution**

The system allows the motorcycle engine to start only when:

✅ The rider is wearing the helmet.

✅ The FSR sensor detects pressure inside the helmet.

✅ Bluetooth communication between the helmet and motorcycle is established.

✅ The motorcycle ECU verifies the authorized helmet signal.

If the rider removes the helmet while riding, the system automatically disables the ignition system for safety.

---

# **⚙️ System Components**

## **1. Force Sensitive Resistor (FSR) Sensor**

* Installed inside the helmet.
* Detects pressure when the rider wears the helmet.
* Sends a signal to the Bluetooth module.

## **2. Bluetooth Module**

* Provides wireless communication.
* Transmits helmet status to the motorcycle unit.

## **3. Battery Module**

* Supplies power to the helmet electronics.
* Ensures uninterrupted operation.

## **4. Electronic Control Unit (ECU)**

* Receives helmet authentication signals.
* Processes safety logic.
* Controls ignition authorization.

## **5. Relay Module**

* Acts as an electronic switch.
* Enables or disables the ignition circuit.

## **6. Fuel Pump Control**

* Supplies fuel only after successful helmet verification.

## **7. Ignition System**

* Starts the motorcycle engine after all safety conditions are satisfied.

---

# **🔄 Working Principle**

### **Step 1 – Helmet Detection**

The rider wears the helmet.

The FSR sensor detects pressure and generates an activation signal.

---

### **Step 2 – Signal Transmission**

The Bluetooth module sends the helmet status to the motorcycle unit.

---

### **Step 3 – Authentication**

The ECU verifies:

* Valid helmet signal
* Authorized Bluetooth connection
* Safety conditions

---

### **Step 4 – Ignition Enable**

After successful verification:

* Relay closes the circuit.
* Fuel pump activates.
* Ignition system becomes active.

The motorcycle can now start.

---

### **Step 5 – Continuous Safety Monitoring**

The system continuously monitors helmet status.

If the helmet is removed:

* Bluetooth signal is lost.
* ECU detects unsafe condition.
* Relay opens.
* Fuel supply stops.
* Engine shuts down.
